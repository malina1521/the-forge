Danny & Rose

BY J & M

8 - Danny

The moment I walk into the room, my eyes find hers. Bewildered. Like she's trying to figure out if I'm real. Three times. There is no such thing as a coincidence, and every atom in my body knows it.

“Good morning everyone. As you have probably gathered, my brother will not be able to make it today, so he asked me here to represent his interests. He has granted me the right to vote on his behalf for today’s proceedings.” I explain calmly.

I turn around the room and start giving orders, not waiting to allow anyone to interject. “Avery, please bring up today’s agenda on the big screen. Catherine, you are our facilitator. Andy, you will log our votes. John, you’re our timekeeper today, please make sure we stay on target. Joining us today is our new event coordinator, Rose Marie Clearwater. You’ll hear from her regarding our winter party. ” I gesture in her direction before continuing. “Today’s meeting is penciled in for two hours, I think we can get it done in an hour and a half. I’d like to take an extended lunch, let’s make it happen.”

Everyone seems to be stunned that I know who they are. I have conferenced into a few meetings recently and every one of these meetings is recorded. Stephen sends me the recordings via email. I watch them to stay informed. Today, I can’t stop fidgeting. All I can focus on is getting through this meeting and what the hell I am going to do about Rose. Or Rose Marie. What a name.

“The first item on today’s agenda is our property in the marketplace district.” Catherine starts. “All the leases for the building are up in six months. Projections show that market value for the area is increasing.”

I start to drown her out. How am I going to handle this? What will I say to her? Conveniently enough, I am supposed to have a private meeting with her in a few hours. Part of me is counting the minutes until I get her alone. The other part knows exactly what will happen when I do. I don’t know how much more I can restrain myself. Ravenous. That’s the word for it. I tried this weekend — found someone at a bar, fully willing, fully capable. Couldn't do it. Didn't even want to. All I want is Rose. I try to keep my attention on the meeting.

“Today we are voting on the proposal to demolish the building and rebuild a shopping mall in its place. You all have ten minutes to look over the proposal and projections. There are two coins in your envelope, green for approval, red for denial. When you’ve made your decision, simply return your envelope to the stack and drop the coin into the box to cast your vote. Questions go on the whiteboard on the left as usual.” Catherine says as she passes out the envelopes. I watch as John sets the timer and the board members each open their envelopes. I open mine and start to read over the numbers. While this looks great on paper, this would mean displacing a huge community of families who live in the apartments. The projections for the initial coat are monstrous and rezoning would be a nightmare. I close the envelope and walk over to cast my vote before taking my seat until the rest of the board does the same. We repeat this agonizing cycle for just over an hour, getting through six of the seven agenda items fairly smoothly.

“The last item is the winter party. To discuss this proposal and budget today, welcome Rose, our new event coordinator.” Catherine says dryly.

Rose stands and walks over to the podium. She sets down her notes. Clears her throat. She is passionate, confident and beautiful. Her hair is pinned up and it does something devastating to her cheekbones.

I watch as the board members nod and laugh as she goes through her proposal. She has managed to captivate the entire room within minutes. I can’t help but be even more attracted to her. She steps away from the podium and heads straight toward me.

“Now, I could go on about some of the fine details and my prospective vendor lists and even share a secondary plan, but Mr. Blacke asked us to make this a speedy meeting. I think we can all agree that this plan is perfect and will make our party one to remember forever. I know the asking number is a lot, I’m willing to cut twenty grand off the request so we can skip any negotiation and get this vote over with. Thank you.” With that, she returns to her chair and takes a seat. The entire room is silent briefly before proceeding to drop their votes. The votes are tallied and she has accomplished a unanimous victory.

“That concludes today’s agenda. Mr. Blacke, unless you have anything else, I motion to end this meeting.” Catherine says.

“I second that motion.” I don't hesitate, though my mind is fixated on a certain beauty in pink. I underestimated her. She's a natural.

“Meeting adjourned.” Catherine says before gathering her things. Similarly, the rest of the room gathers their things and makes their way out quickly. I watch as Rose walks out with Melissa. They laugh at something as they turn the corner. The last few members leave and close the doors behind them. I sit there alone in silence for a few minutes just pondering my thoughts. I can’t bring myself to move.

I grip my phone tight enough to turn my knuckles white, staring at the ringing screen until it finally connects.

I don't give him the satisfaction of a greeting. "Why didn't you tell me, you fucker?"

A heavy sigh bleeds through the speaker. "Jeez. Hello to you too, big brother. Look, I checked your schedule—you had a wide-open day minus golf with Chris. Let’s not pretend I just derailed your life. It’s twenty-four hours. I’ll be back in the city by dinner, and you can go right back to your country club."

“Ha. I wasn’t even mad when I got the call this morning. I know where you are. I’m happy you decided to take the time for you and I hope he is alright.”

“He is. Thank God.”

“Good. Hey, why didn’t you tell me about the event coordinator?”

“First of all, I did tell you when I made the decision to choose her plan. Second, you better not. You don’t get to walk in there and woo her. I saw her first and I have been contemplating how to ask her out for weeks. Damn company policy against fraternization makes this difficult. She's your employee too though. And regardless of that, bro code dude, I saw her first.” The bravado in his tone makes my eye twitch.

“Actually brother, I saw her first. Remember the woman I met in a club six months ago that I told you I couldn’t stop thinking about?”

“Oh no. You’re kidding. Rose is the same Rose you have been having fantasies about for months?” he laughs, stops, then starts laughing again. “I’m sorry.”

He’s not sorry. I may as well come clean. This could get weird. “It gets worse. On Friday night, I went to O’Malley’s. I was alone at the bar and you’ll never guess who walked up right as I was going to order. She used the same line I did verbatim the day I met her to chime in when I was ordering. I thought I was dreaming at first. but then we got to talking. She was supposed to be celebrating a new job with one of her friends but something came up and her friend left abruptly. I decided to take her to dinner. We had a really nice time. I enjoyed it more than I should. Before you ask, nothing happened. I had to meet Gregory at the courthouse so I asked Mark to drive her home. I thought that would be the end of it because I didn’t get her number or anything. I told myself it was probably for the best. Until a few hours ago, I had convinced myself to move on for her safety and mine. When I saw her here today, I realized this is the third time I have run into this woman. The universe is playing a sick sadistic game with me. Now that she’s an employee, she's forbidden fruit.”

“What do you want me to do? I can fire her and find someone else?”

I know he'd do it in a heartbeat. I can't ask him to.

“No. That won’t be necessary. I will handle things today. You better be here tomorrow. I will go back to my life and make a point of avoiding her from here onwards.” I was convincing myself more than him. It costs me something to do this. I can't avoid this woman forever but I can damn well try. I know this is the way it has to be. Her safety matters more.

“If you’re sure.” He responds dryly. I can tell he is already done with our conversation. With everything going on, I don't blame him for being distant. I quickly go over the board meeting details with him, letting him know it all went as planned.

“I will call you tonight and brief you on the day.” I say before hanging up. I glance at the clock and finally make my way out of the conference room. I find a vending machine in a lounge and grab myself a bag of chips before heading into Stephen’s office. I close the doors behind me and find Avery straightening up the files inside one of the cabinets.

“Avery, would you mind calling me in something for lunch?” I ask as I sit down.

“Yes Sir. Would you prefer sandwiches, burgers or Chinese?” She asks. My brother clearly has her trained well.

“Surprise me. Order enough for two, and order yourself something too. Bring it in when it arrives." I say. I watch her nod, stand up to leave. She closes the doors behind her.

I open up my calendar and find the rest of the day is booked with meetings back to back until 6pm. This is going to be a long day. I decide to use the next twenty minutes to nap. Thankfully Stephen has one of those ridiculous long leather sofas in his office. I stretch out and close my eyes.

9 - Rose

I have three minutes until I’m supposed to meet Danny in his office. Or should I say Mr. Blacke's office. What are the odds? I've spent most of my lunch hour staring at a sandwich I couldn't bring myself to touch, running through opening lines and discarding every single one. I still have nothing. I grab my things anyway.

When I reach the office, Avery is not at her desk as she was this morning. The large double doors are closed. I contemplate waiting outside but decide against it. I knock twice before opening the door. "Mr. Blacke?" I call out.

I walk in to find Danny is fast asleep on the sofa in the corner. Absolutely not what I walked in here prepared for. One leg hanging off the sofa, arm shoved under his head like a pillow, snoring. Still in the full designer suit and tie. I don't know what I was expecting but it wasn't this.

I do my best to quietly close the door and make my way to the armchair across from him. I sit and set my laptop, the portfolio, and my notebook down beside me.

"Mr. Blacke?" Several long seconds of nothing. “Mr. Blacke, should I come back later?”

“Rose.” He startles awake and sits up fast, immediately reaching for his tie. “I didn’t realize the time. I must have dozed off. You’ll have to forgive me.”

"It's not a problem. I can have this meeting rescheduled."

“No. That won’t be necessary.” He says. He starts to make his way across the room when I notice a small red dot centering on his chest. The red dot registers before my brain does. I'm already moving — one hand grabbing his lapel, full body weight forward — and we hit the floor together as the window behind us explodes. Glass everywhere. Shouting from somewhere down the hall. I'm already counting exits.

“Fuck! Mr. Blacke, I’m sorry if I hurt you. You’re still in danger. I need you to crawl behind that chair and then towards that door." I hike my skirt up way past my normal comfort and unclip my holster, grabbing my gun from my hip. I slowly rise to peek out towards the direction the shots were fired from. When I turn around, I see Danny behind me. Instead of following my direction, he lifts his pant leg and pulls out a weapon of his own.

He lets out what I can only describe as a multilingual profanity explosion. I catch Italian, something that might be Greek, and at least two words I don't recognize but absolutely understand. Finally he addresses me. "Do not tell me what to do! Let’s get out of here!"

I ignore him and move along the desk, low and slow. Nothing. No movement, no shadow, no second shot. Danny falls into position without being told, flanking the other side. I clock that immediately. He's done this before. He quickly tucks away his weapon and I follow suit. No need for the entire office to know I am packing. Why on earth would Danny have a gun? Why is someone trying to kill Danny? How in the hell is Danny my boss? Will I still have a job after this?

I follow him out of the office and towards an emergency exit. He takes the stairs at a pace I'm not proud to be struggling to match. Finally we get down to the parking garage. You can hear screams and panic all around us. His hand is locked around my wrist and his jaw is set in a way that says we are absolutely not stopping to discuss this. I don’t have time to worry about that as when we finally turn the corner from the stairs, I see a guy with a gun running behind a car. Danny points to Mark who is sitting in the same black town car just across the way.

"Get to that car." He commands.

We step out from the stairwell and I see the shooter take aim just in time to step forward and push Danny aside. Another shot goes off and grazes my thigh. I wince in pain.

"Shit!" I scream. There I go cursing again. I can't help it. I have him. Clean sight line, steady hands. Danny grabs my arm before I can pull the trigger.

"No. Leave him. Let's go." Danny says as he grabs my arm. He holds me down and steers me the other direction, behind a line of cars. We leapfrog forward, one covering while the other moves. I point in the direction of the shooter and Danny nods. We keep our eyes on him as we slowly make our way across, hiding behind cars and poles until we get to Mark.

Mark freezes, his jaw dropping as his eyes dart between our drawn weapons. His own gun is already up, a reflex. Kicking his door open, he begins to climb out of the car.

Danny waves him off. He opens the passenger side rear door and I climb in. He follows and shuts the door.

"Get us out of here. Now!" Danny directs to Mark who immediately pulls the car out and speeds out of the garage.

"Would you like me to take you to the hospital?" Mark casually looks back at us. I guess he noticed I’m bleeding.

"That's not safe. Let me make a few calls. Keep driving. Get us out of town." Danny quickly fires instructions as he pulls out his cell and starts dialing.

I feel a wet spot on my leg and look down. Instantly I feel the pain. Even though it's a flesh wound, I'm bleeding a lot. "Give me your shirt."

He struggles to unbutton the shirt one handed with the phone to his ear before finally just ripping it off. The buttons fly everywhere in the car. Holy shit. He is stronger than I thought and even more muscular. He hands the shirt over without a word. He looks like he belongs on the cover of something. I check myself immediately. Focus, Rose.

Danny hands the shirt to me. I struggle for a second, between the skirt, the holster, the moving car — it takes me a moment to work out the logistics. I unclip my holster and then I rip a long strip of the sleeve of his shirt and start to wrap it around my leg. I apply pressure and create a makeshift bandage, tying a knot to secure it tightly. I need to stop the bleeding. I repeat this process a few times until I'm satisfied. I sit back, look out the window, and try very hard not to think about whose shirt is currently wrapped around my thigh.

"Hello. Do you have what I asked for?" Danny’s tone is cold. I can tell he is seriously upset but I can't make out what the other person on the line is saying. I watch as he visibly relaxes a bit. I can't read his expression but something tells me it's best not to ask.

"Yes. Okay. Keep digging." He pockets the phone, no goodbye.

We drive for a while until we're out on a dirt road. Danny finally looks up from his phone and says, "Pull over. Now."

Mark immediately complies and the car comes to a screeching stop. Before I know what's going on, Danny has a hand on my arm and is pulling me out of the car. He closes the door and he shoves me against the side of the car.

"Who are you really?" He yells as he gets in my face.

"Get your fucking hands off of me NOW!" I scream as I push him back. "I'm Rose, the event coordinator. Who the fuck are you?" I ask. He has no right to treat me this way, I just saved his life and took a bullet for him! This ungrateful son of a bitch. He's trying to intimidate me after all that? He must underestimate me.

"I had a friend look into you. Rose Marie Clearwater didn't exist until eight years ago. You don't have any credit history, no vehicle, no drivers license, no registered address. Not even a lease in your name. You do have a conceal carry license purchased under a false ID with a false address, a library card, a bus pass and a museum pass. Your identity is fabricated, you live under the radar and it's obvious that today was not your first encounter with being shot. You have no history but don't worry, you did a damn good job of covering it up. I'm only going to ask you once more, who the fuck are you? Which agency do you work for?" Danny is shaking. I can tell what he is really asking.

I knew this was coming. I rehearsed for it.

"I was troubled as a teenager. My home life was horrific so I decided to leave. I made a deal with a powerful friend who helped me get a false ID. I left and never looked back."

Thankfully I have four other alternative identities I maintain just in case I have to run again. I can't tell him who my family really is. Finally I settle on sharing the bare basics.

"I left my family and my entire life behind. They are dangerous people. They are still looking for me so I have to take precautions. I am not a fucking cop, I don't work for any of the stupid agencies." I push confidence into my voice. My mind is on my leg and the fact that he just blew through eight years of careful work in an afternoon. I take a deep breath before starting again. "And I definitely don't work for any of your enemies either. I work for you. I saved your life." This has to work. My life is on the line.

"I don't believe you. That's too convenient, sounds like something a cop would say." He grabs my arm again and shuffles me back towards the car. I shrug him off and he steps back and paces, muttering under his breath. It looks like he is having an argument with himself.

"I don't know what else to say. If you don't believe me, you don't believe me I guess. Leave me here. I can take care of myself." Wishful thinking.

Instead he just gets angrier. He continues pacing. I watch him for another minute or two. Back and forth. Six steps to the left, six steps to the right. Finally I work up the courage to speak again.

"What do you want me to say? What can I possibly do to convince you?"

He draws his weapon and turns and walks up to me. He stops inches away and grabs me by the back of my neck and gently angles my head up towards him. We are practically nose to nose as he has this look on his face that speaks more than words ever will. I see so much sadness masked by that anger in his eyes. Finally, he points the gun at my head and speaks again. "Tell me your real name. Look me in the eye and tell me your real name." He says without breaking eye contact.

At this point I can tell another half truth, or I can tell him the real truth. Either way, I know if he doesn't believe me, he won't hesitate to shoot. There are no witnesses besides Mark who is still in the car minding his own business. He has seen through me quite a few times and I know he will call my bluff. I reluctantly decide to tell him the truth.

"Fine." I let out a heavy sigh before speaking again. "I was born as Rose Mary Vasiliki Anastas." It feels strange saying that name out loud. It has been my biggest secret for eight years.

"Anastas. As in related to Gedeon Anastas?" He steps back and drops his hand. Then grabs my arm again before I can move.

"That would be my father." It takes me a moment to finally find my voice. My mind is racing. This is a nightmare! Now I have a whole new set of problems. I didn't expect him to know the name. There are only three reasons Danny would know of my father. The first reason I have already ruled out, he is not a cop, so that leaves the possibility that he works for my father, or that he is my father's competition. Either way, I'm in deep shit. I watch as Danny processes this information. I see his expression change as he contemplates what to do next.

"Get in the car."

I comply, knowing full well I am in no condition to fight him. Even if I was, it would do no good. He is large enough and strong enough, he would most likely overpower me. I scoot all the way over to the other side immediately. He gets in behind me and slams the door.

"Take us to Villa Nero." Of course he already has a plan. Before I have a chance to say anything, he pulls out his cell and hits a button to make a call. It rings twice before an older man answers. "Hello?"

"Hello. I need you to make a house call. Bring a surgical kit." Unlike before, this time when he got in the car, he made a point to sit in the middle seat, much closer to me. I can hear a man on the other end. I assume he is a doctor of some sort.

"I can come by tomorrow or you can come here." The man replies.

"No. Listen to me. Leave now. I just texted you the address. The gate will be expecting you."

"You expect me to just drop everything and--"

"Yes. Now." Danny cuts him off and then hangs up the phone. He turns to me and says, "We have a lot to discuss. No more lies."

"No promises." This is not how I expected this day to go. I sit quietly trying to find anything that would indicate where we are. I look out the window and watch as we drive farther and farther out of the city. We are on one small highway after another. No main roads. I watch the mile markers and estimate we are probably going north.

The rest of the car ride is silent. We drive for what feels like another hour before Mark turns off onto a private road lined with trees. It is built in a spot where it's barely notice-able unless you're looking for it. About a hundred feet in, he pulls out a remote key that he punches a code into. There are armed security guards on the inside. The tall iron gate starts to open. It looks like the fence line goes on for miles. Once we are past the gates the road winds for quite some time as we drive up the side of a mountain. Finally we reach a cleared area among the trees. I am so busy trying to commit every turn we took to memory so I can find my way out alone that I don't realize Danny has been staring at me intently. I wonder how long he has been watching me.

We drive up to a huge castle-like mansion. It's made of old grey stones and has a huge tower on either end. It looks as if its been here for centuries but nothing has deteriorated. The garden around the edges of the house is perfectly maintained. You can see a vineyard in the distance around the back of the property. There is a fountain coming out of some rocks that is surrounded by rose bushes in full bloom. This is not at all what I was expecting. Honestly I didn't know what to expect at all. This whole day has been a surprise.

Mark pulls into a massive carport to the left of the house. He immediately parks and gets out of the car. He makes his way around and opens the door for us. Danny scoots out and then offers a hand to me. I reluctantly take his hand and he gently pulls me out of the car. He proceeds to bend down and pick me up, one arm under my knees the other at my back. He starts carrying me towards the house.

"Put me down. I can walk."

"No." He seems insulted by my request. I don't understand this man. For a split second I consider that I may be a hostage. I decide not to fight him and put that thought out of my mind.

Even when he shoved me against the car, he calibrated it — intimidation, not damage. My lower back would like to file a formal complaint, but I'm otherwise fine. Even when he grabbed my neck, it wasn't harm he had in mind. Conversely, when he had the gun to my head, I knew he was capable of pulling the trigger but chose not to. From what I've gathered, it's safe to say, whoever he is, if he wanted to, he could have killed me in seconds with his bare hands. He chose to be gentle.

He carries me in silence through a side entryway into a small mud room with a beautiful antique hall tree and clothes hooks lining the opposing wall. Most of the hooks are covered with coats and hats. There are several pairs of boots tucked under the hall tree. He hits a few buttons on the keypad on the wall to disarm the alarm system then continues through into a hallway.

The floor is made of gorgeous cream colored marble and there are dark oriental floor runners lining the middle of the hallway. On the left is a huge gold framed painting of some woman. On the right wall a narrow buffet underneath a large mirror. There is a long curved staircase made of iron and oak at the end of the hallway opposing a pair of giant antique doors that are rounded on the top. The doors are beautiful, they're made with a matching oak frame that surrounds a stained glass pattern. As we approach them, I am mesmerized by the way the light reflects through the different color pieces onto the light colored marble.

He breaks my trance as he smugly asks, "You like my house?"

"You live here?" Who is this man? Just how much money does he really have? I knew that Blacke Corp was successful, but this is beyond that.

"Sometimes." He opens the doors and brings me into a massive sun room. He sets me down in a blue chair and turns to look out the window.

"It's beautiful. Where are we?"

He said no more lies. I am going to do my best to trust him. But I need to know where I am. I need a way out. I can't tell him everything. I shouldn't trust him at all. My father would be laughing at me right now. He taught me to be smarter than this. I don't know what's gotten into me.

He chuckles and turns to face me. "It's safer if you don't know. I can assure you you're still in California and I will arrange for your return safely once we're through with this."

"Through with what?"

He ignores my question. "A doctor is here to see your leg. I need to find a shirt. Wait here." He turns and leaves the way we came in.

I look down at my leg. My makeshift bandage did its job to slow the bleeding and start clotting the area, but the wound still aches terribly and the shirt is bright red. The adrenaline has worn off and I am really feeling the burn. I try to put it out of my mind and start to help myself up to my feet. If he can't tell me where we are, maybe a landscape or something nearby will give me a clue.

As I secure my footing and start to distribute my weight, I feel really light headed and I lose my balance. I reach out for something as my knees bow and I end up using the chair to settle myself on the ground. My tailbone slams onto the floor with a thud and I just start laughing. I'm so totally fucked. This is like the worst day ever and it just keeps getting worse. I am stupid to think that Danny will let me leave here. I should be terrified of the man.

I continue laughing as I replay the sequence of events in my head. I danced with him at a club. I went to dinner with him. He turns out to be my boss. I saved his life. He forces me to give him my real name. He knows who my father is. He wants to keep me here and won't tell me where we are. And the worst part, I actually like Danny. I'm still attracted to him. Even with all this shit, I feel safe. Stockholm Syndrome?

Danny walks in and frowns. "I left you in the chair. Why are you on the floor?" He’s visibly annoyed. Without warning he bends down and picks me up and helps me into the chair in one sweeping motion.

I see a man walk in behind Danny. He is older, probably in his fifties. He has glasses and a salt and pepper beard. He smiles at me and makes his way over. The closer he gets, the more I realize that he is really short, probably five foot tall at most. He is wearing a classic tailored suit with a fedora and carries a small leather briefcase in what I presume is his medical kit.

"Hello, you must be Rose." He extends a hand.

"Hello." I shake his hand and smile.

"Danny tells me you got hit and that you dressed the wound yourself in the backseat of his car."

I nod and pull my skirt up slightly by the slit to show him my thigh. He is going to see my tattoo. I hope he doesn't ask. Such an embarrassing drunken decision.

"From the looks of it, you were successful in slowing the bleeding." He examines my leg. He bends down and sets his briefcase down next to him. "May I?"

"Yes. I used square knots to tie it off. If you pull here," I pull out an end that I had tucked in, "they will come loose fairly easily." I undo the knots one by one. I remove the last of the blood soaked cloth slowly and grimace as the wound is exposed to the open air.

"Oh dear. That will definitely require some stitches. Let me help you." He turns and opens his briefcase. He pulls out a ton of sterile bandages, cotton pads, wipes, alcohol, and a small stitching kit with a few of those new age surgical glue "easy" stitches. He gets to work immediately. He starts by cleaning it. "This will hurt." He speaks just before he uses the alcohol to clean the wound.

"Hello, captain obvious," I think to myself. This shit already hurts. I take a deep breath in and let my mind wander. Son of a bitch. Fuck. Holy shit. Wow. This hurts like hell. I continue to try to focus my mind. It took years of practice but if I concentrate hard enough, I can make my mind go elsewhere and forget the pain entirely. This was one of many lessons I learned from my father over the years.

I concentrate on my breathing and I don't move a muscle, focusing on slowing my heart rate. I look down to watch as he works. He looks up at me curiously as if in disbelief that I am not showing any signs of pain as he presses into the wound. I know the exact moment he sees the butt of my gun strapped to my other leg. He moves like he is about to say something but decides against it. He doesn't say a word.

I can tell he wants to ask about what happened and even who I am but he is holding back. It's obvious he is paid to be discreet and not ask questions. He is clearly scared of Danny. Danny didn't go far. He is sitting in the chair across from me doing something on his phone. He is more curious than anything about me.

After thoroughly cleaning it, the doctor measures the wound and decides he will need to manually stitch a few stitches and then he can use eight of the glue stitches in order to secure it. He explains as he goes and with my prior training, I know exactly what to expect.

He searches for a numbing agent in his bag. "It's okay. I don't need it." He looks at me curiously and keeps going. He inserts the needle skillfully and secures the first pass and then another. He has a unique approach where his stitches align at an angle.

Once he finishes with the clear stitches, he starts with the glue stitch pieces. He sets them up expertly side by side, sticking each piece carefully with his applicator tool. Once they are all in place, he starts to pull the wound together slightly to link them together. I can tell he is doing his best to be delicate. I have always wanted to use this type of stitch but it's insanely expensive and isn't commonly found in the world. I learned how to stitch myself up the old fashioned way. I have watched a few videos on how this technology works on YouTube though. I can't help myself as he does this. "May I?"

He pulls back and motions for me to take over. I take the tools and I finish interlocking the stitches and then pull the tabs to seal the wound closed. It aligns perfectly. Proud of my work, I smile again. He returns my smile and then starts opening some sterile bandages. I let him finish wrapping my leg to seal the area.

"You really didn't need me at all." The doctor says as he packs up his supplies. He leaves some extra gauze and bandage tape for me and tells me to replace it tomorrow. He locks his briefcase before standing and waves goodbye. He leaves us without another word the same way he came in.
