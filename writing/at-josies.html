At Josie's (Jason's Version)

Watching her from across the room was like mapping a complex system of pure intent.
She was studying the dark canvas with a fierce, quiet concentration, completely ignoring the ambient chatter of the evening crowd.

When I stepped up beside her, the air felt charged, stripped of all standard background static.
"The contrast is what holds it together," I muttered, looking at the painting but tracking her reaction. "Without the deep shadow, the light wouldn't have an architecture to cling to."
