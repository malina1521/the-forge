It Wasn't Even A Date

JAKE & NORA'S STORY

Jake

When Mike asked me to pick up his girl and show her around, I didn't know what to think. Mike and I haven't talked much since we graduated, but he knew I've never left Boston, so he called and, just like old times, he sweet-talked me into doing what he wanted. I still owe him a few favors from our college roommate days, and curiosity about Mike's new girl got the best of me. Mike explained that Nora is here for a conference this weekend, and he didn't want her to be alone in the city. She's staying at the Graduate Hotel, and I am now five minutes late picking her up for dinner. Of course, I've never met Nora, so I have no clue what to expect. In college, Mike usually went for the classic girl next door types, mostly blondes, but who knows now?

When the cab pulls into the valet drop-off area, I tap my phone to pay before I step out. After a few seconds, the familiar beep sounds. I thank the driver before heading in. The Graduate hasn't changed much on the outside, but I am surprised to find that the inside is not clad in the old-world luxury decor I remembered. The lobby is decorated in minimalistic black and white decor, and they've moved to a modern glam look. I take a minute to look around, remembering how different things were the last time I was here before I make my way towards the restaurant.

Grabbing a seat at the bar, I order a soda and pull out my phone again. I have a text from an unknown number from a few minutes ago.

"I'm running late, see you in ten."

I sit there and watch the people around me, enjoying the calm ambiance with the lights overhead. The slow jazz tunes playing over the speakers add a nice touch of sophistication. I scan the room, focusing on each set of people as I go. There are a few men in suits at a high table behind me discussing a merger deal, and an older couple that looks to be on a date sitting at the other end of the bar. I'm pulled back from my thoughts when the scent of sweet strawberries overwhelms my senses, sending a wave of arousal coursing through my veins. I turn around just in time to see a dark-haired beauty taking the seat next to me.
