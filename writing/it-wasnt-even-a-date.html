It Wasn't Even A Date (Jake & Nora)

They sat at a corner table beneath a flickering neon sign, two cups of cold coffee sitting between them like standard props in a poorly rehearsed play.
"We're just coordinating projects," Nora said, adjusting her glasses and looking directly at her laptop screen. "Purely structural logistics."

Jake smiled, leaning back against the worn leather booth. "Right. Standard operations. That completely explains why we've been sitting here talking about sci-fi world-building for three hours past our deadline."
Nora closed the tab, but didn't pack up the computer. The noise of the diner faded into a comfortable background hum, neither of them in any hurry to break the unmapped alignment.
